Pigeonhole Principle:
This principle has many different variation. Here is two of them.

1. If K+1 pigeon must go in K boxes, then at least a box contain at least 2 pigeons.
2. If aK+1 pigeon must go in K boxes, then at least a box will contain
at least a+1 pigeon.

This principle is used to prove whether a arrangement is possible or not.
